The Sacred Frequency Studio

A complete guide to building transformative sound for The Candle — from a single healing tone to a full production bed.

01

What Is This Tool

The Sacred Frequency Studio is a browser-based tone generator built specifically for The Candle channel. It synthesizes healing frequencies in real time using your device's audio engine — no downloads, no plugins, no cost.

It combines two ancient tuning traditions: the 432 Hz natural tuning system (as opposed to the modern 440 Hz standard) and the Solfeggio frequency scale — a set of tones referenced in Gregorian chant and sacred music going back centuries.

The Tartarian frequencies (marked with a ✦) are the deeper, more speculative layer — resonance points associated with pre-modern sacred architecture and lost acoustic knowledge. Use these with intention.

Everything it generates can be recorded and used directly in your videos.

02

Getting Started

1
Put on headphones
This is not optional for binaural beats. The left-right channel separation that creates the brainwave entrainment effect only works through headphones. Speakers merge the channels and the effect disappears. For pure tone playback (no binaural) speakers are fine.
2
Select a root frequency
Click any card in the frequency grid. The number is the Hz value — the number of vibrations per second. Your selection becomes the foundation everything else is built on. Start with 432 Hz (marked in amber) if you're new to this.
3
Press play
The large circle button starts and stops sound. You'll see the visualizer respond and the status bar change to show your active frequency. Sound fades in over two seconds — this is intentional. Sudden tones are jarring.
4
Adjust master volume and reverb
The two sliders flanking the play button control overall volume (left) and reverb mix (right). More reverb creates the sense of a large sacred space — a cathedral, a cave, a stone chamber. For intimate recordings, keep it around 30–40%. For expansive sleep content, push it to 70–80%.
5
Explore and trust your body
There is no wrong choice. Move through frequencies slowly. Some will feel neutral. Some will feel like something. Pay attention to that. What you find resonant is what you should use — your response to it will carry through the recording.

04

Using Layers

A single tone is thin. Layers create the harmonic richness that makes a frequency feel like an environment rather than a sound effect. Each layer is a mathematical relationship to your root frequency.

🎵
Root Drone — The foundation. Always leave this on. It is the frequency you selected.
🎵
Octave Above — Exactly double the root. Adds height and breadth without introducing dissonance. Safe to always use alongside the root.
🎵
Perfect Fifth — 1.5× the root. The most consonant interval in Western and sacred music. Adds warmth and completeness. This is the "cathedral" layer.
🎵
Sub Undertone — Half the root frequency. Felt more than heard. Adds physical weight and grounding. Essential for sleep content and prayer beds. Keep it low — 20–30%.
🎵
High Shimmer — Four times the root. An ethereal overtone that creates the sense of light or presence above the sound. Use sparingly — 10–15% max. Beautiful for sacred reading backgrounds.

Each layer has its own volume slider. The goal is not equal volumes — it's a blend with a center of gravity. The root should always dominate. Everything else serves it.

05

Binaural Beats

A binaural beat is an auditory illusion. When your left ear hears 432 Hz and your right ear hears 438 Hz, your brain perceives a pulsing beat of 6 Hz — the difference between them. That 6 Hz pulse is in the Theta brainwave range, which the brain associates with deep meditation and intuition.

This is not metaphor. This is neuroscience. But it only works through headphones. Always note "use headphones for binaural effect" in your video descriptions.

Recommendation

For most of The Candle's content, Theta (6 Hz) is the right choice. It supports the meditative, liminal state that defines the channel's aesthetic — not fully asleep, not fully alert. The threshold space.

06

Waveforms

The waveform determines the shape of each oscillation — which determines the timbre, the texture, the feeling of the sound.

〜
Sine — Pure. A single frequency, no overtones. The most "clinical" and also the most sacred. Used in healing frequency work almost universally. Start here.
△
Triangle — Warmer than sine. Adds gentle odd harmonics that give the sound a slight wooden or organic quality. Good for contemplative content where sine feels too sterile.
⋀
Sawtooth — Rich and complex. Full harmonic series. This is the "buzz" — more intense, more present. Use when you want the frequency to be felt, not just heard. Use sparingly for The Candle's aesthetic.

For Production — Recording Your Audio

The studio generates live audio in your browser. To use it in your videos, you need to capture it as a recording. Here are three methods from simplest to most professional.

🖥️
Method 1
Screen Recording
QuickTime (Mac) or OBS (PC/Mac). Record screen audio while studio plays.
🎙️
Method 2
Audio Interface
Route output through Audacity or GarageBand loopback for cleaner capture.
📱
Method 3
CapCut Import
Record as audio file, import directly into CapCut alongside your footage.
The Candle Production Stack

Layer three audio elements in CapCut: (1) studio-generated frequency bed at 40–50% volume, (2) Suno ambient composition at 15–20% volume, (3) your voice narration at 100%. This is what gives the channel a sound that cannot be replicated.

Record your frequency bed for at least 3–5 minutes longer than your video — you will want flexibility in the edit. A 10-minute video needs a 13–15 minute audio bed recorded.

When you switch to a new video type, change the root frequency. Let 174 Hz be your grief/healing signature, 432 Hz your morning/neutral signature, 528 Hz your transformation signature. Over time, returning viewers will feel the difference before they consciously hear it.

The Suno Prompts

Section V of the studio contains five ready-to-use Suno composition prompts — one for each of The Candle's content categories. Hit COPY on any prompt and paste it directly into Suno's style or lyrics field.

🎼
Style Prompt — Goes in Suno's "Style of Music" field. Describes instrumentation, frequency references, tempo, texture, and mix behavior.
📜
Mood / Direction — Goes in the lyrics or secondary prompt field. Gives Suno an emotional and spiritual reference point. The more specific the image, the more coherent the output.

Generate 3–5 variations per prompt and listen with the frequency studio running underneath. Find the Suno track that sits most naturally in that tonal space. That pairing becomes your video's sound identity.

Important

Always use Suno's instrumental mode (no vocals). The Candle's voice is yours. No other voice belongs in the space.

Safety & Disclaimers

These notices exist to protect you as a creator and to honestly serve your listeners. Please read them, embody them, and include the short-form version in every video description and on any page where this studio is hosted.

The Core Position

The Candle creates sound environments for spiritual and contemplative use. It does not diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any condition. This is not a retreat from the belief that sound is sacred and powerful — it is the accurate and honest framing of what sacred sound is.

🩺
Not a substitute for medical or psychiatric care. Listeners experiencing physical illness, mental health crises, trauma responses, or grief should be supported by qualified professionals. These sounds may complement care — they do not replace it.
⚠️
Epilepsy & seizure sensitivity. Binaural beats involve rhythmic audio stimulation that may trigger seizures in susceptible individuals. Always note in your video descriptions: "If you have epilepsy or a seizure disorder, consult your physician before using binaural audio."
🚗
Do not use while driving. Every video description for sleep or meditation content should include: "Do not listen while driving or operating machinery." This is standard practice in the category and expected by listeners.
🧠
Mental health sensitivity. Deep relaxation and altered states can surface difficult emotions — grief, fear, old wounds. This is not a malfunction. But listeners in acute distress deserve a note in your description pointing to support resources if needed.
🔊
Volume. Prolonged exposure above 85 dB causes permanent hearing damage. These sounds work at low volume. Say so. "Use at a comfortable, low volume. These frequencies do not need to be loud to be effective."
🫃
Pregnancy. Low-frequency tones and binaural beats have not been studied in pregnancy. Recommend listeners who are pregnant consult their healthcare provider before extended use.

A Note on Intent

Frequencies are not magic. They are physical phenomena — pressure waves moving through air at measurable rates. The science of some healing frequency claims is contested. That is honest to say.

What is not contested: that sound affects the nervous system. That rhythm and drone induce altered states. That sacred music has served human spiritual life for as long as humans have existed. That your listener's body will respond to a careful, intentional sound environment differently than it responds to noise.

The studio is a tool. You are the instrument. What you bring to the recording — the prayer, the stillness, the belief that this matters — is what transmits. The frequencies support that. They don't replace it.

Record when you are present. Don't record when you are distracted or performing. Listeners feel the difference even when they can't name it.
